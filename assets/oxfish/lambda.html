Testing ITQ Valuations

Ernesto Carrella

August 19, 2015

While ITQ trading is still not enabled, Id like to test the ability of fishers to evaluate correctly a quotas worth.
The main weakness of the ITQ system in the NETLOGO prototype and the Tofts paper it originates from is that fishers do not accurately discount for expiration date of the quotas.

Take a fisher who has to decide whether or not to buy some fishing quotas. If \(\Pi_u\) is the profit per unit of fish caught and \(\lambda\) is the quota price per unit of fish, then the value of an additional quota is:
\[ \text{Value of Quota}= \begin{cases} \Pi_u - \lambda,& \text{if quota is used}\\ -\lambda, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}\] That is, there is the risk of buying a quota and not using it in time which result in the fisher just wasting \(\lambda\)$.

The expected value of buying a quota (for a risk neutral fisher) is then: \[ E[\text{Value of Quota}] = \text{Pr}(\text{Needed})(\Pi_u - \lambda) + \text{Pr}(\overline{\text{Needed}})(- \lambda) \] And we can solve for the \(\lambda^*\) which is the price that makes fishers indifferent between owning or not a quota \[ \lambda^* = \text{Pr}(\text{Needed})\Pi_u \]

Now if we define: \[ \begin{align*} q &= \text{Quota owned} \\ c_d &= \text{Daily catch} \\ t &= \text{Day of the season} \\ T &= \text{Season length} \end{align*} \] Then the probability that you are going to use a unit of quota you buy is equal to the probability that: \[ c_d *(T-t) > q \] That is that catches per day times days left in the season is higher than the number of quotas you have or: \[ 1 - \text{Pr}(c_d \leq \frac{q}{T-t}) \] And if we assume that \(c_d\) is normally distributed then we are basically saying that \[ \lambda^* = \left( 1 - \Phi(\frac{q}{T-t}) \right) \Pi_u \]

I let fishers use moving averages to predict \(c_d\) and \(\Pi_u\). We can then ask them what is their estimated \(\lambda^*\) as if they currently held \(q=1000\) quotas of fish. What follows is the time series of estimated \(\lambda^*\) for one fisher in our agent-based model:

There are a few results in this plot.

  • Quotas drop in value towards the end of the year (this is because it becomes less and less likely they can be used)
  • If the fisher is having bad luck (as the one shown does periodically for the first 4 years) the reservation price for ITQs can be 0
  • Even though \(c_d\) and \(\Pi_u\) are predicted by moving averages, \(\lambda^*\) can vary dramatically through the years. This is due to the biology, the exploration and the gear parameters of the model
