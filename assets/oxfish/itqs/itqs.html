Two Species ITQ

Ernesto Carrella

October 9, 2015

Here I test the effects of the newly implemented multi-species ITQ quota system.

The biology set up is as follows: there are 2 species of fish, specie 0 (the red one) is abundant, specie 1 (the blue one) is rare but they coexist in every cell of the map. The ratio of biomass is about 70 red to 30 blue.

The red fish is worth 10$ a unit, the blue fish is worthless.

There is an ITQ system in place. Yearly each fisher gets 4500 units of quotas for red and 500 units of quotas for blue. The proportion of quota then is 90 red to 10 blue, unbalanced compared to the biomass ratios.
The following results emerge:

  • Blue quotas, even though blue fishes are worth 0$, are very expensive as they are needed to keep catching reds
  • By contrast the red quotas, even though reds are the target species, are worth only a small proportion of the landing price they command (as one needs to discount the cost of buying blue quotas).
  • Blues act like choke species: 100% of blue quotas are redeemed while only 30% of red quotas are
  • Red quotas price start high at the beginning of the year and then drop as the season unfurls. Blue quotas start high at the beginning of the year, get progressively higher and are over before their price ever drops.
